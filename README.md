## What Is Retatrutide?

Retatrutide is a once-weekly injectable peptide that simultaneously agonizes three receptors: GIP (glucose-dependent insulinotropic polypeptide), GLP-1 (glucagon-like peptide-1), and glucagon. This triple mechanism is what separates it from tirzepatide (dual GIP/GLP-1) and semaglutide (GLP-1 only).

In Phase 2 trials, retatrutide produced up to 24% body weight reduction over 48 weeks — the highest ever recorded for a non-surgical intervention in clinical history. For context, tirzepatide peaked around 20-21% in SURMOUNT-1.

The compound is also known as:
- **Reta** (common shorthand)
- **LY3437943** (Eli Lilly internal designation)
- **GLP-3R agonist** (informal triple-receptor nickname)

## Why I Switched from Tirzepatide to Retatrutide

I ran tirzepatide for about 14 weeks. Results were solid — dropped around 18 lbs, appetite was noticeably suppressed, no major sides. But I kept reading about the Phase 2 retatrutide data and the glucagon component specifically. The glucagon receptor engagement drives energy expenditure in a way that GIP/GLP-1 alone does not. That is the piece tirzepatide is missing.

The trade-off is that glucagon also drives more nausea at higher doses, so the titration schedule with retatrutide is slower and more deliberate.

## FAQ

**Is retatrutide FDA approved?**
No. As of 2026, retatrutide (LY3437943) is in Phase 3 trials. It is not approved for human use. This log documents research use only.

**What is the difference between retatrutide and semaglutide?**
Semaglutide (Ozempic/Wegovy) is a GLP-1 receptor agonist only. Retatrutide adds GIP and glucagon receptor agonism, making it a triple agonist with significantly higher weight loss potential in trials.

**Retatrutide vs CagriSema?**
CagriSema (cagrilintide + semaglutide) combines amylin + GLP-1 agonism. Phase 3 data shows ~22% weight loss. Retatrutide Phase 2 showed up to 24%. Different mechanisms, similar ballpark results.
